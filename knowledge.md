## 1. What is EcoHarvest?

EcoHarvest is described as a regenerative finance ecosystem connecting agriculture, renewable energy, and investment through trading-style systems, harvest pools, and blockchain-related positioning, aimed at global adoption and measurable environmental impact.

**Public tagline:** “Built From Vision, For The World.”

**Three pillars (marketing):**

- Agriculture meets innovation — tokenizing production and regenerative farming across food systems.
- Energy meets sustainability — linking renewable infrastructure with blockchain-oriented investment.
- Investment meets real-world value — capital that compounds with communities in mind.

**Positioning:** Not limited to one geography; Africa is framed as part of a broader mission (impact and opportunity), with regional narratives for United States, Europe, Middle East, South America, India, broader Asia, and Africa.

## 2. EcoToken and EcoHarvest.org (whitepaper themes)

**Mission:** Make ecological credits and measurable environmental impacts more accessible; connect capital to vetted green initiatives with transparency.

**Problem framing:** Large renewable energy sector but many viable projects lack funding due to slow, opaque traditional finance; investors want high-impact opportunities on trustworthy platforms.

**Solution framing:** EcoToken powers EcoHarvest.org as a decentralized-style platform where users fund tangible projects (examples cited in materials: solar in Arizona, wind in Denmark). Tokenization is used to argue traceability of funds and alignment of environmental outcomes with financial returns.

**Blockchain (documented intent):** Binance Smart Chain mentioned for infrastructure; smart contract themes include staking, governance, and reward distribution.

**Token utility (documented intent):**

- Staking and yield (example cited in pitch materials: ~8% annual yield from project revenues such as solar sales — illustrative, not a promise for any live product).
- Governance / DAO voting on funding initiatives.
- Marketplace uses: carbon credits, green NFTs, sustainable product purchases.
- Community grants for eco-friendly startups.
- Buybacks and burns described as mechanisms for scarcity and stability (design intent).

**Tokenomics buckets (high level):** Fundraising and capital raising (~30–40%), incentives and rewards (~30–35%), governance and community growth (~10–15%), team and development (~10–15%), utility and usage (~15–20%), partnerships and ecosystem (~10–15%), buybacks and burns (~5–10%). Exact on-chain parameters must be confirmed from official token documentation at launch.

**Vesting themes:** Team and advisors locked 12–24 months with incremental release; investors 6–12 months; staking and rewards released gradually over time.

**Roadmap themes:** Smart contract development and audits, private and seed rounds, community building, public sale (ICO/IDO/IEO), DEX and CEX listings, staking and DAO activation, NFT marketplace, carbon integrations, enterprise and geographic expansion, marketing phases (pre-launch hype, sale period, post-launch adoption).

**EcoBridge (concept):** Described as a decentralized asset bridge to tokenize eco-assets; references to Scan.ecotoken.earth as an explorer for EcoTokens, NFTs, and transactions; EcoCredits as internal ecosystem currency with NFT and carbon-trading angles.

**Important:** Whitepaper and deck content is strategic and forward-looking. The live platform may implement features gradually; not every documented item may be on-chain or live yet. Confirm on [https://ecohavest.org](https://ecohavest.org) and official announcements.

**Whitepaper PDF:** [https://ecohavest.org/ecoharvest-whitepaper.pdf](https://ecohavest.org/ecoharvest-whitepaper.pdf)

## 4. Official FAQ

Published at [https://ecohavest.org/faqs](https://ecohavest.org/faqs).

**How can I get started with EcoHarvest?**  
Create an account, complete KYC verification, and make your first deposit. Support: support@ecohavest.org.

**How do I apply for KYC verification?**  
After you are a verified customer, you get access to EcoHarvest services. Submit clear color copies (photo or scan) of: proof of identity (passport, national ID, or driving license — if the ID shows your correct residential address, proof of address may not be required); proof of address (bank/card statement or utility bill such as water, gas, electric, internet, telephone; or residency certificate or tenancy contract).

**Are there any withdrawal limits?**  
For cryptocurrency withdrawals, the stated rule is that the ordered payment amount must be equivalent to at least 50 USD.

**How long until a deposit appears?**  
Deposits are processed promptly; the user receives a notification when the balance updates.

**What is EcoHarvest’s approach?**  
EcoHarvest is a regenerative finance ecosystem uniting agriculture, energy, and investment through intelligent trading systems, Harvest Pools, and blockchain technology for global adoption and real-world impact.

**Getting started steps (FAQ page):**

1. Get access — sign up at [https://ecohavest.org/sign-up](https://ecohavest.org/sign-up).
2. Investment — from the dashboard, deposit funds, then choose a product aligned with your goals.
3. Earn returns — returns as specified for the selected product.

## 8. Platform capabilities (behind the website)

The live product is backed by authenticated APIs used by the web app. Capabilities include, in summary:

- **Identity and compliance:** Sign-up, sign-in, KYC submission and review workflow.
- **Balances and wallets:** Multi-currency balances; user wallet addresses; crypto deposits to platform-provided addresses and withdrawals to user-specified addresses.
- **Funding rails:** Crypto plus manual or instruction-based flows for bank, PayPal, and Cash App (subject to approval and status in the dashboard).
- **Investments:** Configurable investment plans; user investments with profit and schedule fields as defined in the product.
- **Transfers:** Internal and user-to-user transfers with approval where applicable.
- **Referrals:** Referral tracking and rewards as configured.
- **Store:** Product catalog with categories.
- **Crowdfunding:** Pools, minimums, participant targets, referral requirements for eligibility, and related settings.

Exact behavior, currencies, and limits are defined in the live app and admin configuration, not in this document.

## 9. Token lifecycle and go-to-market (reference)

**Pre-ICO:** Idea, whitepaper and tokenomics, legal structure, MVP, community, token deployment, private sale, pre-sale.

**ICO:** Public sale, distribution (airdrop or claim).

**Post-ICO:** DEX then CEX listing, vesting, price discovery, product delivery, ecosystem growth, governance, scaling.

**Simplified pipeline:** Idea → whitepaper → community → private sale → pre-sale → ICO → distribution → listing → build → adoption → long-term value or failure.

**Planning priorities:** Strong whitepaper and tokenomics, credible team visibility, demonstrable product, community channels, clear token supply, allocation, and vesting before fundraising.
